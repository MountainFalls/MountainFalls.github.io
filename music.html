Mountain Falls is a multi-instrumentalist who primarily focuses on guitar and voice. Fingerstyle classical and electric guitar and complex rhythms characterize Mountain’s melody driven music. Mountain records and produces their own tracks in Ableton Live 10 and is currently working on their first album to be titled Journal. Ivy, their first single for this album, is now out on all streaming platforms.

In Mountain’s hometown they often walked in the forest near the top of the town hydroelectric dam. The white noise produced by the cascading water and spinning turbines produced a slightly tonal hum that could be heard throughout the entire section of forest. One day while listening to this omnipotent sound Mountain thought of how the dam was an intersection between the natural world and the technology of humanity. The turbines transformed the force of the chaotic flow of the river's current into organized streams of electrons that bring life to lightbulbs, and cell phones and automatic doors. Inspired by this thought, Mountain sampled the sound of the dam, of the rain on the metal walkway that turned forest to industrial zone, of their feet on that walkway and of their knuckles wrapping on the maintenance door. From a single kick drum and augmenting and arranging those samples Mountain created a piece of music titled From Water.
